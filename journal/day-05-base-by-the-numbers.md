# Day 5 — Base by the Numbers

## The Stats That Shouldn't Exist

For a chain that launched in August 2023 with no token incentives and no airdrop farming, Base's dominance across L2 metrics is remarkable.

### Market Share (as of early 2026)

| Metric | Base | Rest of L2s Combined |
|--------|------|----------------------|
| **TVL (Total Value Locked)** | ~46.6% | ~53.4% across 50+ chains |
| **Fee Revenue** | ~62% of all L2 fees | ~38% split among all others |
| **Active Addresses** | ~70% of L2 active addresses | ~30% across all others |

## Why This Is Unusual

There are **50+ competing L2 networks** on Ethereum. Many of them have:

- Their own token with farming incentives
- Massive airdrop campaigns to attract users
- Millions in ecosystem grants

Base has none of that — and still captures nearly half the TVL and the majority of active users.

## What's Driving It

1. **Coinbase integration** — seamless onramp from the largest US exchange
2. **Real app activity** — not just DeFi farming, but social apps (Farcaster), NFTs, and consumer use cases
3. **Low fees** — sub-cent transactions make microtransactions viable
4. **No token distortion** — activity reflects genuine usage, not airdrop hunting

## Key Takeaway

Base's numbers prove that distribution and real usage beat tokenomics and incentive programs. Nearly half the L2 market with zero token incentives is unprecedented.

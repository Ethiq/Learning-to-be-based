# Day 4 — Strategic Independence from the Superchain

## Background

Base was originally built on the **OP Stack**, the open-source framework that powers Optimism and many other L2 chains in the "Superchain" ecosystem.

## The Big Move

In February 2026, Base announced it was **moving to its own Unified Stack** — effectively leaving the Superchain.

**Reasons cited:**

- Faster development velocity
- Full control over the technical roadmap
- No dependency on external teams for critical upgrades

## Why This Shook the Ecosystem

The numbers tell the story:

- Base was generating **97% of Superchain revenue**
- It was sending **15% of its revenue** to Optimism as part of the Superchain agreement
- After the split: that funding stopped
- **OP token dropped ~25%** on the news

Base was essentially subsidizing the entire Superchain. Once it left, the economics of the remaining ecosystem changed dramatically.

## What This Means

This move signals that Base is confident enough in its own engineering and user base to go independent. It's a bet that Coinbase's resources and Base's traction are enough to sustain an independent L2 without the shared infrastructure benefits of the Superchain.

It also raises questions about the viability of "shared L2 ecosystems" when one participant dominates the revenue.

## Key Takeaway

Base leaving the Superchain was the biggest L2 ecosystem event of early 2026. It showed that distribution and revenue matter more than shared infrastructure.
